# PMF Outreach List · US Manufacturers & System Integrators (leads-us)

> Target segment: US small/mid control system integrators (SIs) and manufacturers hit by the controls/automation engineer shortage.
> Pain evidence (public): ~400,000 unfilled US manufacturing roles on average in early 2025; controls-engineer searches routinely stretch **past 60 days**; firms report spending **25–35% more** on contract engineers; 62% of automation projects face 6+ month delays for lack of engineering talent. Sources: [BLS/AMTEC workforce report](https://www.amtec.us.com/blog/manufacturing-workforce-report), [Area Development](https://www.areadevelopment.com/skilled-workforce-STEM/q4-2025/the-workforce-bottleneck-in-americas-manufacturing-revival.shtml), [Reshoring Initiative 2024 report](https://reshorenow.org/content/pdf/2024-1Q2025_RI_DATA_Report.pdf).
> TalEngineer fit: SIs and reshoring plants use vetted, escrow-protected overflow controls engineers to clear backlog without a 60-day hire. Positioning mirrors talengineer.us — *Verified by AI. Protected by escrow. Matched in 48 hrs.*
>
> **Sourcing discipline**: every row carries a public link — CSIA membership pages, Control Engineering / Plant Engineering award listings, or the SI's own site. No unsourced entries. Segment is dominated by SIs because they publicly document hiring pain and have named leadership on LinkedIn (most reachable channel).

## Priority key (reachability)

- **P1** — Small/mid CSIA-certified SIs with clear regional footprint + named principals → most likely to subcontract vetted overflow talent through the platform.
- **P2** — Larger/multi-office SIs and award "Giants" → longer procurement, but active hiring pain.
- **P3** — Enterprise-scale integrators (>$300M) → included for completeness; less likely to use a marketplace.

Contact channels are **public prospecting entry points** (company site, LinkedIn, CSIA directory), not verified private contacts.
